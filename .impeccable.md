## Design Context

### Users
Crypto users and hackathon judges who need to *feel* that a phishing approval cannot move their funds. They arrive skeptical, short on time, and want one undeniable demo: sign → quarantine → drain fails → panic.

### Brand Personality
**Focused · urgent · precise.** Voice like a locksmith, not a SaaS marketer. Confidence without neon cyberpunk. Tagline stays: “Sign anything, lose nothing.”

### Aesthetic Direction
Reference: [clawpump.tech](https://clawpump.tech/) — bold stacked headlines, dark canvas, numbered flows, one primary CTA, product-first sections, no decorative wallpaper noise.
Anti-references: purple-on-black AI dashboards, Inter + glow, starburst backgrounds, chip salads, soft “fintech cream.”
Theme: dark industrial vault — steel neutrals, signal amber for quarantine, green for blocked/safe, blood-red only for panic.

### Design Principles
1. **Demo is the product** — the first connected view is a numbered attack path; everything else is secondary.
2. **One job per section** — hero sells; path proves; dashboard manages.
3. **Brand over headline fluff** — Deadbolt reads as the hero signal; copy stays short.
4. **Accent is rare** — white primary CTAs; amber/green/red only for state; no purple brand wash.
5. **Addresses are tools** — monospace for hashes; human labels (“Demo drainer”) beside them.
